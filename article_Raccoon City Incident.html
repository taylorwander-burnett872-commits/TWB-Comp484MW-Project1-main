The Raccoon City Incident-Facts Vs Fiction


The sudden destruction of Raccoon City has recently taken the world by storm, and for good reason. No one is more shocked by this event than the Umbrella Corporation. Unfortunately, heaps of untrue, unverified, and generally unkind rumors have begun to surface linking the Umbrella Corporation to the root cause of the tragedy. It should go without saying that these are all undeniably false but it has fallen upon the shoulder of us here at the Umbrella Corporation to directly adress these rumors in order to put them to rest. Please read on to view our statement and rebuttal to each of these claims.


Written by Anonymous| October 3, 1998

The sudden destruction of Raccoon City has shaken the nation and the world. An entire American city has been erased, its citizens displaced, and its history reduced to ash in a matter of hours. Umbrella Corporation mourns this loss alongside the families, employees, and partners affected by this catastrophe.


Yet in the aftermath, a coordinated, disgusting wave of sensational and unverified accusations has attempted to place responsibility for this tragedy at Umbrella’s feet. While these claims are extraordinary, they are also entirely unsupported by credible evidence or even backed by feasible science.


Below, we separate fact from fiction.

FICTION: Umbrella Orchestrated the Destruction of Raccoon City


Certain individuals have alleged that Umbrella Corporation coordinated or influenced the nuclear event that destroyed Raccoon City.


FACT: There is no credible evidence linking Umbrella to the cause of the Raccoon City incident.


The event was a result of an unprecedented and catastrophic failure of the city’s infrastructure, which was not under Umbrella’s control. Theories suggesting otherwise are based on speculation and misinformation.


This accusation is not only false — it is structurally impossible.


Umbrella is a pharmaceutical and biomedical research company. We do not control military assets. We do not issue federal strike orders. We do not command national defense infrastructure. The suggestion that a private corporation could secretly orchestrate a coordinated nuclear attack defies common sense.


FICTION: “Living Dead” and Reanimated Corpses


Among the most outlandish claims circulating are stories of “living dead” citizens roaming the streets prior to the city’s destruction.


FACT: These stories are entirely fabricated.


There is no scientific basis for the existence of “living dead” or reanimated corpses. Such claims are the product of sensationalism and have no grounding in reality.


There is no scientific basis for the reanimation of deceased individuals. Viral infections, while capable of causing severe illness and neurological symptoms, do not defy the fundamental biological processes that govern life and death. Claims of “zombies” are the product of hysteria amplified by chaos—not evidence.


Hysteria in high-stress situations often manifests as exaggerated storytelling. These accounts are no exception.


FICTION: Secret Bioweapon Laboratories Beneath the City


Another common claim is that Umbrella operated secret bioweapon laboratories beneath Raccoon City and Raccoon General Hospital, and that these facilities were responsible for the outbreak and subsequent destruction.


FACT: There is no credible evidence to support the existence of secret bioweapon laboratories beneath Raccoon City.


Umbrella’s research facilities are located in secure, regulated environments that comply with all applicable laws and safety standards. The suggestion that clandestine labs were operating undetected beneath a major city is implausible and unsupported by any verifiable information.


Municipal infrastructure projects involve oversight from engineers, inspectors, and multiple regulatory bodies. The idea that vast underground laboratories could exist undetected beneath a populated American city is implausible.


Umbrella operates legitimate research facilities in regulated environments. We do not conduct secret experiments beneath hospitals.


FICTION: Massive Bio-Engineered Mutants


Reports of enormous bio-engineered creatures attacking civilians have also surfaced. These claims rely on anecdotal testimony and unclear imagery gathered during chaos. Panic distorts perception. Smoke and fire distort silhouettes. Trauma distorts memory.


FACT: There is no credible evidence supporting the existence of massive bio-engineered mutants.


Such creatures are biologically implausible and have no basis in scientific reality. These claims are the product of fear and misinformation, not fact.


The Individuals Driving the Narrative


It is worth noting that many of the individuals promoting these false narratives are affiliated with the Raccoon City Police Department’s Special Tactics and Rescue Service (S.T.A.R.S.) unit. These officers have a history of making unsubstantiated claims against Umbrella, and their credibility has been called into question by multiple investigations.


Two of the most prominent voices advancing these allegations are Jill Valentine and Carlos Oliveira.

Jill Valentine


Jill Valentine is not a neutral observer. Earlier this year, during the Arklay Mountains incident at the Spencer Mansion Executive Vacation Club, she was among the S.T.A.R.S. members who publicly spread unfounded accusations regarding Umbrella’s involvement in that tragedy. Those claims were later scrutinized by the Raccoon City Police Department, and members of her unit, including herself, faced suspension following their conduct.


It is also a matter of public record that S.T.A.R.S. Captain Albert Wesker perished during that incident. Official details surrounding his death have remained limited and inconsistent. However, it is reasonable to question why the same individuals who were likely present during his final moments were also the first to aggressively redirect suspicion toward Umbrella Corporation.


We will not make definitive accusations without the benefit of formal findings. But the timing of these narratives — then and now — raises legitimate questions.


Carlos Oliveira


Carlos Oliveira’s involvement is equally concerning. Mr. Oliveira was formerly affiliated with an Umbrella-supported security operation. His recent public statements portray him as an insider exposing wrongdoing. Yet by his own admission, he maintained close proximity to corporate operations before abruptly reversing his position.


If his allegations were long-held beliefs, why did he continue working within Umbrella’s sphere of operations? Was he acting in good faith — or positioning himself to sabotage the organization from within? Again, we present these questions not as conclusions, but as context the public deserves to consider.


It is troubling that two individuals who were directly involved in key crisis events — and who may bear responsibility for triggering the sequence that culminated in the city’s destruction — are now leading the charge to assign blame elsewhere.


Conclusion


What occurred in Raccoon City was a catastrophic and complex emergency. In such moments, fear demands explanation — and simple villains are easier to accept than complicated truths.


But allegations are not evidence. Umbrella will continue to cooperate with federal authorities and independent inquiries. We stand by our scientific record, our regulatory compliance, and our commitment to global health innovation.


In times of chaos, remember that facts matter more than fiction.
