S.T.A.R.S Members Suspended Following Arklay Mountains Investigation


The destruction of Umbrella's Arklay Mountains Executive Vacation Club was an unfortunate tragedy felt all across the company. Out of the disaster, a series of bad-actors from the Raccoon City Police Department subdivision of S.T.A.R.S took the opportunity to propagate some nasty rumors in regards to the facilities destruction. Now, following an investigation into those claims, the RPD has announced the well-deserved suspension of said agents. Let us take the opportunity to refute their claims once again and provide some clarity on why we think those S.T.A.R.S agents began to attack Umbrella in the first place.


Written by Anonymous| August 17, 1998

The recent destruction of Umbrella Corporation’s Arklay Mountains Executive Vacation Club was a profound loss for our organization and the many employees and guests who cherished the retreat. The facility, designed as a private corporate getaway promoting rest, team-building, and executive planning, was reduced to ruins under circumstances that remain under active review by local authorities.


In the wake of the incident, several members of the Raccoon City Police Department’s Special Tactics and Rescue Service (S.T.A.R.S.) unit, namely, Chris Redfield, Jill Valentine, Barry Burton, and Rebecca Chambers, publicly advanced serious allegations against Umbrella Corporation. These statements, made without verifiable evidence, rapidly circulated throughout the community and national press.


Following an internal investigation by the RPD into the conduct surrounding those claims, the department has announced the suspension of the officers involved.


Umbrella welcomes the RPD’s commitment to accountability and professionalism. Public servants carry the responsibility of ensuring that statements made in uniform are grounded in fact. In moments of crisis, speculation and rumor can cause unnecessary panic, damage reputations, and distract from the real work of recovery and rebuilding.


Adressing the Allegations


Let us once again make it clear: the Arklay Mountains Executive Vacation Club was a privately owned recreational property. It was not a research installation, testing facility, or site of any experimental activity. Assertions to the contrary are categorically false.


Umbrella’s scientific operations are conducted in fully regulated environments under strict oversight. Conflating a vacation property with corporate research facilities demonstrates either a profound misunderstanding—or an intentional effort to redirect scrutiny.


A Question of Motive


It is also worth noting that the Arklay incident resulted in the reported death of S.T.A.R.S. Captain Albert Wesker. Official accounts regarding Captain Wesker’s final moments have been limited and, at times, inconsistent.


We extend our condolences to Captain Wesker’s colleagues and family. However, given the gravity of the accusations directed at Umbrella, it is reasonable to ask why certain members of S.T.A.R.S. were so quick to shift public attention toward our company in the immediate aftermath.


When tragic outcomes occur during tactical operations, questions naturally follow. Were procedures followed appropriately? Were command decisions sound? Could errors in judgment have contributed to the loss of life and destruction of property?


Rather than allowing those questions to be examined, several S.T.A.R.S. officers instead chose to promote dramatic and unsubstantiated narratives regarding Umbrella’s involvement.


We will not speculate further. We simply believe the public deserves transparency and facts—not rumors born from chaos.


Moving Forward with Facts


Umbrella Corporation remains committed to cooperating fully with local authorities. We are conducting our own comprehensive review of the events surrounding the Arklay property and will continue to support efforts to uncover the truth. With that being said, we would like to now address directly the members of S.T.A.R.S Involved:


Tragedy should never be used as a shield against accountability.


As investigations proceed, we are confident that clarity will prevail, and the unfounded claims made against Umbrella will be recognized for what they are: attempts to distract from unanswered questions elsewhere.


Umbrella stands by its record of innovation, integrity, and service to the Raccoon City community and we hope that through all the noise, the good people of Raccoon City are able to hear the truth.
